About

The Pristine Project is an initiative birthed by an individual burdened with the concern of accelerating advocacy on menstrual health/hygiene and all its associated issues. Since its conception, we have reached more than 1,000 girls over a period of 24 months; equiping them with critical knowledge on menstrual hygiene management, fostering an entreprenerial mindset in the girls, while also providing menstrual care items and other hygiene materials.


Read More

Menstrual hygiene management, according to UNICEF, is described as the process where adolescent girls use a clean menstrual hygiene management (MHM) material to absorb or collect blood that can be changed in privacy as often as necessary for the duration of the menstrual period, using soap and water for washing their bodies as required and having access to facilities to dispose of used menstrual management material.

The Pristine Project emphasizes the urgency of this public health issue while also highlighting the transformational power of improved menstrual hygiene to empower the world's women and girls and unlock their economic and educational potential for relevant opportunities.

Empowering women and girls to unlock educational & economic potential

Preparing the girl child mentally and psychological for the menstrual phase

Help women and girls practice optimal menstrual hygiene

Providing health care and hygiene materials such as sanitary pads



Globally, 52% of women and girls are of reproductive age - around 1.9 billion people. Yet, overwhelming stigma still surrounds the topic of menstruation, and it is often difficult for females in many countries to practice optimal menstrual hygiene.
