About Us

The Pristine Project is an initiative birthed by an individual burdened with the concern of accelerating advocacy on menstrual health/hygiene and all its associated issues. Since its conception, we have reached more than 1,000 girls over a period of 24 months; equiping them with critical knowledge on menstrual hygiene management, fostering an entreprenerial mindset in the girls, while also providing menstrual care items and other hygiene materials.

We believe that the girl-child has a critical role to play in the society and therefore needs to be equiped with resources to thrive amidst various socio-economic limitations. Thousands of Nigerian girls welcome the arrival of their inevitable monthly period with fear. Twenty-five percent of girls lack adequate resources for menstrual hygiene management. Globally, over 500 million women and girls lack adequate facilities for menstrual care. Inadequate WASH (Water, Sanitation, and Hygiene) facilities have consistently posed a major challenge to girls and women.

Meet the Convener

With little or no clue of what those first streaks of blood meant and the many implications that come with it, I was neither mentally nor psychologically prepared for that phase. Naive and unprepared, I journeyed into a new chapter seemingly clueness of the woman I had now become.

Kemi Ogunsanya is a core Literacy Educator by profession, a teacher who is passionate about attaining success with the children she works with, and is equally dedicated to making positive impacts within her sphere. Beyond the classroom, she is passionate about advancing the cause and rights of the girl child and work with various initiatives to achieve this.
