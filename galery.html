Ebute-Metta Outreach

Amidst the sudden realities of Covid-19 and its resultant effects, The Pristine Project successfully empowered 100 female teenagers in a rural community in Ebute-Metta, Lagos with health care and hygiene materials such as sanitary pads, sanitizers, disinfectants, hand washers, and face masks.

This project also achieved its core goal of equiping the girls with critical entrepreneurial skills to enable them thrive financially by teaching them how to make some types of soaps and sanitizers.

A survey carried out by our team with local communities in Ebute-Metta, Lagos revealed alarmingly that a major percentage of young girls within these local communities can neither afford sanitary pads nor get the funds for their menstrual care from their parents or guardians as a result of their challenging economic status. This was a clarion call for us, and a bell of hopr for these girls.


Makoko Outreach

This outreach was focused on menstrual hygiene education, skill acquisition on producing basic hygiene products, and distributing free sanitary wares and hygiene products.

100 teenage girls and young women participated in this outreach.


Igando Outreach

This event convened more than 500 female teenagers in Igando High School, Lagos. The girls were educated about menstrual hygiene education, entreprenership, and leadership. Free sanitary pads and menstrual hygiene materials were also distributed.

This event was done in partnership with DIVA PADS
